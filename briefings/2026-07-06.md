#### Threat Context
A critical Remote Code Execution (RCE) vulnerability has been reported in Palo Alto Networks PAN-OS. This type of vulnerability allows an unauthenticated attacker to execute arbitrary code on the affected system, typically with high privileges. A successful RCE exploit on a network device like a firewall can lead to complete compromise of the device, enabling an attacker to bypass security controls, gain full control over network traffic, establish persistence, exfiltrate data, or pivot into the internal network.

#### Why It Matters to Our Stack
This threat is highly relevant to our organization because Palo Alto Networks PAN-OS is one of our configured stack products. An RCE on our firewall could have catastrophic consequences, giving attackers a strategic foothold in our network perimeter. We collect 'firewall_traffic_logs', which are crucial for detecting exploitation attempts targeting the firewall and for monitoring unusual outbound traffic initiated by a compromised firewall. 'edr_process_events' from Microsoft Defender for Endpoint will be vital for detecting post-exploitation activities on internal systems if the attacker pivots from the compromised firewall. This includes identifying suspicious process execution, network connections, or file modifications on endpoints. While 'cloudtrail' and 'email_gateway' logs are less directly related to the initial PAN-OS RCE, they remain important for detecting subsequent attacker actions, such as attempts to access cloud resources or initial phishing vectors that might have led to an administrative credential compromise used to access the firewall.

### 2. New Credential Harvesting Campaign using Phishing Kit X (BLOG-101)

- **Grounding:** No CVE ID to verify.

#### Threat Context
The threat advisory details a new credential harvesting campaign that leverages sophisticated phishing kits. These kits are designed to mimic legitimate Microsoft 365 login pages, aiming to trick users into divulging their credentials. The campaign specifically targets cloud environments, indicating a focus on gaining unauthorized access to cloud-based services and data once credentials are stolen. Threat actors typically distribute these phishing links via email, often employing social engineering tactics to increase the likelihood of a victim clicking the malicious link.

#### Why It Matters to Our Stack
This threat is highly relevant to our organization given our use of Microsoft services and cloud infrastructure, coupled with our logging capabilities. Our 'email_gateway' logs are crucial for detecting the initial delivery of phishing emails, allowing us to identify suspicious sender behaviors, malicious links, and potentially weaponized attachments before they reach end-users. Microsoft Defender for Endpoint, part of our 'microsoft_defender_for_endpoint' stack, can monitor endpoint activities ('edr_process_events') for users interacting with phishing links, such as browser processes connecting to suspicious domains or any subsequent file downloads. Our 'firewall_traffic_logs' from 'paloaltonetworks_pan-os' can help identify outbound connections to known or newly registered phishing infrastructure. Most critically, 'cloudtrail' logs, and specifically Microsoft 365 logs ingested via 'CloudAppEvents' in Microsoft Defender, are essential for detecting post-compromise activity, such as suspicious logins to Microsoft 365 from unusual locations or IPs, attempts to change MFA settings, or unauthorized access to cloud resources, which 'amazon_guardduty' could also flag for AWS-specific activities. The compromise of Microsoft 365 credentials could lead to broad impact, including data exfiltration, business email compromise, and further lateral movement.

#### Hunt Queries
**KQL:** *Hunts for suspicious emails attempting to impersonate Microsoft login pages by checking common phishing subject lines and URLs that mimic legitimate Microsoft domains but may contain subtle differences or redirect to suspicious TLDs.*
```
EmailEvents
| where Subject has_any ('Action Required', 'Security Alert', 'Password Expiration', 'Verify Your Account')
| where Urls has_any ('microsoftonline.com', 'outlook.office365.com', 'sharepoint.com', 'login.microsoft.com')
| where not (Urls contains 'microsoft.com' and Urls contains 'login.microsoft.com' and Urls contains 'secure.outlook.com') // Basic exclusion for legitimate links
| extend PhishLikelihood = case(
    (Urls contains '.ru' or Urls contains '.cn' or Urls contains '.xyz') and not (Urls contains 'microsoft.com'), 'High',
    UrlCount > 10 and not (Urls contains 'microsoft.com'), 'Medium',
    true, 'Low'
  )
| where PhishLikelihood != 'Low'
| summarize count() by SenderFromAddress, Subject, Urls, RecipientEmailAddress, PhishLikelihood
| order by PhishLikelihood desc
```
